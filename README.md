Walmart Retail Analytics Dashboard

A retail vendor analytics dashboard built in Power BI using synthetic POS data modeled after real Walmart Scintilla / Luminate Data Portal exports. Built to demonstrate practical supply chain and retail analytics skills in a vendor management context.


What This Is

Walmart vendors live and die by their POS data. Every week a vendor analyst pulls a Scintilla export and answers four questions for their buyer:


Are my items selling?
Are they on the shelf when a customer wants them?
Where is performance strong vs weak geographically?
Do I have the right inventory in the right place?


This dashboard answers all four questions across three pages built on a star schema data model in Power BI.


Dashboard Pages

Page 1 - Executive Summary

The 30-second view. Four KPI cards, a 52-week POS trend line showing seasonal patterns, average instock % by item, and a performance breakdown by district.

Page 2 - Sales Detail

The weekly business review view. Week over week POS trend with WoW % comparison line, POS dollars by subcategory, and interactive slicers for item and district filtering.

Page 3 - Instock & Inventory

The operational action view. Store-level instock table with conditional formatting (red below 90%, yellow 90-95%, green above 95%), weeks of supply by item with overstock and OOS risk callouts, and average instock % by district.


Data Model

Star schema with one fact table and three dimension tables:

Dim_Store (1) ──────► (*) Fact_POS
Dim_Item  (1) ──────► (*) Fact_POS
Dim_Calendar (1) ──► (*) Fact_POS

Fact_POS -- Store x Item x Week grain with POS Units, POS Dollars, On Hand Units, In Transit Units, In Warehouse Units, Instock %, and Sell Through %

Dim_Store -- 90 stores across one Walmart region, 8 states, segmented by Volume Tier (High/Medium/Low) and District

Dim_Item -- 40 SKUs across 5 subcategories with realistic replenishment health scores driving instock variance

Dim_Calendar -- 52 weeks of weekly data (Jan 2024 - Dec 2024)

Data Generator

The synthetic dataset is generated by generate_walmart_pos.py and produces a realistic flat-file extract mimicking what you would pull from Scintilla.

Realistic behaviors baked in:


Seasonal index lifts Q4 (holiday) and suppresses Jan/Feb
Volume Tier multipliers drive store-level velocity differences
6 problem SKUs with persistent low instock (60-84% range)
8 problem stores that drag down instock across all items
Combined store/item health scores drive replenishment frequency and OOS probability so all three metrics move together consistently


To generate your own dataset:

bashpip install pandas numpy faker
python generate_walmart_pos.py

Output is walmart_pos_data.csv at roughly 187,000 rows (90 stores x 40 items x 52 weeks).
